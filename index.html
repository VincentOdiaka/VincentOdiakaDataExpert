Prediction of Hepatitis C Virus: A Comparative Analysis of Machine Learning Algorithms Using Python

Hepatitis C is a liver infection that poses challenges in early detection and lacks available vaccines. To address this, non-invasive biomarkers and imaging techniques have been used for diagnosis. This project evaluated five machine learning algorithms to predict the presence of Hepatitis C, identifying six key blood serum markers: "AST," "BIL," "GGT," "CHE," "ALB," and "ALT" as important predictors. Among the algorithms, the Random Forest algorithm performed the best with 96% accuracy, 92% specificity, and 96% precision, making it the recommended model for Hepatitis C prediction.

Microsoft Power BI-Driven Approach to Balance Demand and Supply at Cazolo Pizza

Cazolo Pizza has been facing operational challenges, including frequent stockouts, overproduction of certain pizza types, and difficulties with staff availability. To address these issues, Power BI was used to analyze detailed pizza order data, providing valuable insights into sales patterns, pizza preferences, and peak business hours. The analysis revealed that Veggie pizza contributed the most to revenue, accounting for 26.91% of total sales. However, the variation in revenue contributions among the four pizza categories was minimal, suggesting a balanced customer preference across different types. In terms of size, large pizzas were the most popular, generating $375.32k in revenue, far outpacing other sizes. Cazolo’s busiest time of day was identified as between 1200 GMT and 1300 GMT, indicating a need for optimal staff scheduling during this period. Furthermore, the pizzas in highest demand were the Classic Deluxe, Barbecue Chicken, The Hawaiian, Pepperoni, and Thai Chicken. Based on these insights, it is recommended that Cazolo Pizza optimize inventory management to prevent stockouts, improve demand forecasting to avoid overproduction, and adjust staffing levels to better align with peak demand times.
